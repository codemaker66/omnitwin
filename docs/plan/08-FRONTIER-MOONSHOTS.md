# 08 · Frontier — Moonshots Beyond the Plan

10 July 2026 · New ideas not already in 00–05, at "no guardrails on ambition." Each entry: the idea · the display invention · lane (**Now** = fits current waves · **Next** = post-L2 · **Research** = R&D lane). Claim doctrine still applies to all of them — ambition in capability, never in certainty. Your repo already invents in this register (the gold **evidence beams** anchored to floor points are a moonshot that shipped); this list continues that lineage.

---

## 1. The Concierge — the venue's mind, embodied
A venue-specific intelligence that *lives in the room*: a warm point of light that walks the floor, answers by voice or text ("can we hang florals from the beams?"), and cites its sources — venue rules, past events, review gates — through Proof. Staff teach it by talking; every answer is claim-guarded and provenance-tagged. Display invention: the answer *happens in space* — ask about candle policy and the relevant zones glow with the rule attached. **Next** (the RAG + rules substrate exists in schema today; the embodiment is staging on top of presence-light rendering).

## 2. Total Recall — institutional memory as ghosts
Scrub *backwards* through the room's history: every past event's layout as layered translucent memories. "Show every wedding head-table placement ever used" → a heat-ghost of positions with outcomes attached (flip actuals, guest counts, issues). The data flywheel made visible at the moment of decision. Display invention: time scrubbing that goes both directions — the Timeline's past is as real as its future. **Next** (needs post-event learning data accumulating from first operated events — start capturing now, per 04 §5.3).

## 3. On-site Overlay — the plan standing in the room
A hallkeeper points a phone/tablet at the physical room; the device localizes against the splat (image-to-splat relocalization) and the plan overlays reality: brass arrows to where each table goes, green as zones complete. Setup becomes a guided AR experience with zero markers. **Research** (splat relocalization is an active field; prototype against Reception Room; the payoff — ops error rate near zero — justifies a standing R&D slot).

## 4. Event Cinema — every plan premieres
One click renders a 30-second cinematic trailer of the planned evening: Theatre.js camera paths through the phases, FOH serif titles ("The Hamilton Wedding · September 14"), the room morphing ceremony→dinner→dancing, ending on the couple's saved POV. Clients share it; every share is venue marketing with the product's name in the corner. Display invention: the *plan as film* — no competitor can render what they never captured. **Now-adjacent** (auto-cinematography heuristics + phase morphs are waves E–F outputs; the trailer is an export pipeline on top).

## 5. Live Pulse — the room watching itself (day-of)
On event day the Floor becomes a mimic board: ops tasks light zones green as completed, supplier check-ins (QR at the loading door) appear as arrivals, issues pin to space, the phase playhead tracks *actual* time against plan. The wall iPad in the office shows tonight's room, alive. Display invention: the plan and the day converge on one canvas — planned vs actual as two playheads. **Next** (ops/task/issue schema exists; this is a rendering + mobile-events layer).

## 6. The Constellation — the year as a night sky
Reframe the calendar: the venue's year as a field of lights — confirmed events burn brass, holds flicker amber, dark nights glow faint violet (unsold inventory made emotionally visible to directors). Drag an enquiry onto a dark night to test the fit; conflicts arc between nights that share crews or flips. Display invention: utilization as *presence and absence of light*, not grid cells. A director-brain view no venue tool has ever shipped. **Next** (calendar data exists; this is a view, not a system).

## 7. Weather — the whole venue flowing
Multi-room dollhouse of the entire building with the week's events flowing through it like weather systems: load-ins as fronts arriving, room flips as pressure changes, corridor collisions visible where two events' logistics cross. Ops sees the *building*, not a list. **Next** (needs multi-room runtimes — G10 feeds it room by room).

## 8. The Approval Theatre — buying as ceremony
The client's approval is choreographed like the product's best moment, because it is: the proposal's room settles, house lights dim one notch, their signature draws in brass light across the plan, and the room "locks" with a felt settle — planner and client both watching, live. Peak-end applied to the *revenue event itself*. **Now-adjacent** (proposal share + approve flow exists; this is choreography on top — one build card).

## 9. Light & Sound Studies — the physics of atmosphere
Sun-position scrubbing ("your 6 pm September ceremony light" through the real window geometry) and acoustic auras (speech-intelligibility contours rippling from the stage during the speeches phase). Both labeled *Simulated — planning study*, both selection-aware in Proof. Display invention: atmosphere as scrubbable overlays, honest about being models. **Research→Next** (sun math is cheap and near; acoustics later).

## 10. Neural Staging — decor try-on, honestly
"Show it dressed in autumn florals / candlelit / gala black-tie" → generative restyling of *stills* from the captured room, always watermarked **Generated visual concept — not captured**, never in the planning canvas, only in mood panels of proposals. The ArtiFixer lane productized with the claim guard as its cage. **Research** (the differentiation isn't the generation — everyone will have that — it's the *honest framing* nobody else will bother to build).

## 11. Walk-In — the proposal you can stand inside
WebXR view-only mode: the couple at home in a Quest/Vision Pro walks their actual wedding at guest-eye, follows the planner's laser during a call, sits at grandma's seat. View-only keeps it feasible (no editing physics); Safari 26.2's WebXR-on-WebGPU and Quest's browser make it a weekend prototype against Reception Room. **Next**.

## 12. The Open Ledger — trust as a public number
Venues opt in to publish their operational truth: flip-estimate accuracy, checks run per event, review-gate completion, planned-vs-actual timings. A public trust page — "this venue plans with evidence" — that becomes the category's badge and Venviewer's moat-as-marketing. Nobody can fake it without the evidence runtime that produces it. **Next** (the data exists the moment post-event learning starts; publishing is policy + one page).

## 13–16 · The get-weird annex (added 10 Jul 2026, on request: max creative)

**13 · The Thousand-Year Ledger.** Heritage venues sell history; make it spatial. Scrub the Timeline *past* today: Trades Hall's room rendered through its eras — layered from venue archives, paintings, and records, each layer labeled *historical reconstruction — archival sources*. A couple books the room where guilds have gathered since 1794, and can *stand in that lineage* during their proposal. No competitor can copy a building's soul. **Next** (content project more than code; the Timeline just learns to go backwards).

**14 · The Venue Dreams.** When nobody is planning, the dollhouse idles as ambient art: anonymized guest-flow traces from past events drift through the rooms as slow aurora. On the office wall screen, the building remembers. Staff culture artifact, lobby jaw-drop, zero new data — it replays what post-event learning already stores. **Next** (a render mode over existing replays; privacy: aggregate paths only, never individuals).

**15 · Entrance Rehearsal.** The single most emotional ten seconds of any wedding — the entrance — gets its own instrument: choreograph the doors, the walk line, the guest sightline sweep, the light cue, then preview it from any seat (whose view of the entrance is blocked by a column? fix it now, not on the day). Ties Timeline + seat view + light study into one goosebump. **Now-adjacent** (a phase template + POV choreography once Waves E–F land).

**16 · The Almanac.** Auto-generated per-room book: "your room's light, every month at every hour" — sun-study renders through the real window geometry, bound as a beautiful FOH artifact venues hand to photographers and couples ("September, 6 pm — this window, this gold"). Labeled *simulated lighting study*. A sales asset that compounds the capture moat. **Research→Next** (sun math is cheap; splat exposure grading is the R&D bit).

## Sequencing sanity (the one guardrail that stays)

Moonshots earn their slot only by feeding the wedge: 4 and 8 amplify the proposal loop (revenue), 5 and 3 deepen ops lock-in, 2 and 12 compound the data moat, 6 and 7 sell to directors, 1 ties the knowledge system together, 9–11 are R&D lanes with honest labels. Nothing here front-runs G1–G8 — a moonshot demoed on a fake room would violate the only doctrine that makes the rest believable.
