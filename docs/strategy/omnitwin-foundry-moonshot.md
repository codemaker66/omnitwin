# OmniTwin Foundry moonshot strategy

**Decision:** build full prospective independence now, preserve a lawful vendor-export bridge for historical captures, and make the product a measured multi-representation Foundry rather than a splat converter.

## North star

An operator can bring every authorized source they possess, obtain an honest inventory and processing plan, repair missing acquisition evidence through guided recapture, produce measured and visual representations in one canonical venue frame, compare them at fixed views, and publish an immutable reviewed package without being locked to a scanner, reconstruction vendor, runtime codec or cloud.

Independence means:

- raw and calibration data for future captures are in documented, operator-controlled formats;
- manifests, transforms, provenance, quality gates and job state are OmniTwin-owned;
- algorithms are replaceable workers rather than the schema;
- every provider can be replaced without changing scene truth;
- vendor assets are adapters and never the sole metric authority;
- generated appearance is useful but never mislabeled as captured evidence.

## Strategic verdict

| Horizon | Verdict | Boundary |
|---|---|---|
| Historical PortalCam raw projects | Partially feasible | open sidecars and exports are useful; original sensor payloads and calibration require an official route and written rights |
| Historical venue salvage | Feasible with bounded engineering | align entitled PLY/mesh/poses/SOG/SPZ to independent E57 and add owned photography |
| Future open capture and reconstruction | Feasible with bounded engineering | documented LiDAR/images/video/IMU/control and calibration bundle |
| Universal “supreme” quality | Research programme, not a launch promise | fair held-out evidence must select per-region representations |

## Strategic invariants

1. Raw sources are immutable and mounted read-only.
2. A transform is not trusted because a render looks plausible; reviewed evidence and residuals are required.
3. Geometry, appearance, physics, semantics and export authority may belong to different assets.
4. “Open code” is insufficient: code, weight, dataset, source asset, patent, service and output rights are screened separately.
5. Accuracy is purpose-bound. Research, visual, planning, public and premium profiles have different explicit gates.
6. No provider- or vendor-specific identifier enters the canonical scene model.
7. A failed or blocked stage must explain the next safe action.
8. Active recapture is preferred to hallucination whenever the venue can still be observed.
9. Historical archaeology has a time box; prospective open capture cannot wait for a proprietary parser.
10. Existing Reconstruction Foundry remains the downstream private review/sign/publish boundary.

### Backend recommender policy

The recommender is deterministic policy over declared evidence, not an autonomous authority. It:

- filters mechanisms by requested purpose, source-accuracy tier, asset-specific rights, calibration, camera/path connectivity, hardware, time and budget;
- returns one recommended graph plus materially different alternatives, each with mechanism, licence/source posture, evidence required, expected gain, likely failure and falsifier;
- records excluded candidates and the reason rather than hiding them;
- never changes a rights decision, reviewed transform, quality result, JobSpec intent, approval, authority map or publication state;
- requires a human plan selection plus separate trusted `FoundryRightsApproval` and short-lived `FoundryExecutionConfirmation` capabilities bound to the exact JobSpec subject.

The v0 manifest remains intentionally all-purpose and fail-closed for global approval. Purpose-aware execution is implemented separately: every JobSpec stage binds non-empty `rightsPurposes` to its exact inputs, and `validateFoundryJobRights` fails closed for missing, incomplete or disallowed rights. This may permit reviewed commercial-internal geometry work while still blocking model training, redistribution or public release; prose cannot override the decision.

## Geometry programme

The venue control frame comes from surveyed control where available, otherwise the highest-quality registered metric cloud with an explicit uncertainty class. Room frames are reviewed transforms from that venue frame.

Run these geometry lanes on identical frozen inputs:

| Lane | Role | Strength | Known weakness |
|---|---|---|---|
| LiDAR TSDF | planning/collision shell | stable metric fusion, controllable resolution | memory, thin surfaces, occlusion |
| Screened Poisson | visual/architectural candidate | smooth complete surfaces | closes holes inventively; parameter sensitive |
| Photogrammetry MVS | detailed/textured candidate | high-frequency ornament | scale drift, glass/specular/repetition |
| Neural implicit | research detail candidate | smooth learned surface | metric drift, compute, licence/model risk |
| 2D Gaussian/surfel | research surface candidate | appearance/surface coupling | immature extraction and licence landscape |
| 3DGS mesh extraction | visual candidate | uses appearance model | floaters and non-authoritative geometry |
| Hybrid LiDAR + photo | default supreme candidate | metric frame plus detail | calibration and fusion complexity |
| Manual correction | bounded exception | fixes heritage/operational defects | cost and provenance discipline |

Outputs are separate: room boundaries and semantic topology; measured mesh; planning mesh; collision mesh; navmesh; architectural mesh; LODs; uncertainty field. Watertightness is required only for consumers that need it.

## Appearance programme

Compare, never conflate:

- rights-approved XGRIDS export as historical benchmark/base;
- independent gsplat and 3DGRUT on the same approved cameras;
- calibrated high-resolution photo fusion;
- exposure/white-balance and view-dependent compensation;
- base visual layer plus hero micro-splats;
- base visual layer plus PBR hero meshes;
- residual radiance over measured geometry;
- day/night and lighting states as distinct source groups;
- environment maps for lighting, not geometry;
- generated cinematic output as a separate package.

Use true held-outs; prohibit training-view screenshots as evaluation. Preserve source-master SH and conventions before testing compression.

Fixed-view evaluation is necessary but insufficient. Freeze versioned camera definitions and connected paths: image/pose IDs, camera model, source resolution, crop/exposure, keyframes, path segments, room-graph/loop connectivity and collision/nav authority. Report temporal artifacts and disconnected coverage separately from isolated-frame scores.

## Sensor fusion programme

Every session records clock domain, timestamp quality, camera model, intrinsics, distortion, readout/rolling shutter, IMU biases, camera↔LiDAR↔IMU extrinsics, units, axes, quaternion ordering and covariance. Calibration artifacts are content-addressed and versioned.

Pipeline:

1. validate files, clocks and calibration bundle;
2. calibrate/fix intrinsics including fisheye >180° validity;
3. estimate time offset/readout and deskew;
4. run visual, LiDAR and inertial odometry candidates;
5. factor-graph loop closure and session alignment;
6. align to surveyed/metric venue control;
7. propagate residual/covariance to room and asset transforms;
8. require human review before an alignment becomes authoritative.

Repeated walls, glass, mirrors and chandeliers are explicit failure classes. Walls/floors cannot dominate ICP without stratified evaluation.

## Semantics and active recapture

Machine proposals cover rooms, doors, windows, walls, stairs, lifts, bars, stages, furniture, fireplaces, chandeliers, artwork, power/AV, service routes, restricted zones and heritage features. Each node has label version, confidence, source views/assets, geometry scope, model/version and review state.

The same uncertainty field serves four consumers with different policies:

- recapture selects real observations;
- human review prioritizes risk;
- generated derivatives may fill only masked cinematic regions;
- runtime tiers spend detail where it matters.

A recapture prescription includes frame position in the venue/room coordinate system, height, direction/target, focal length or camera mode, shot count/arc, lighting state, reason, predicted metric improvement and a confidence interval. Measured post-capture gain feeds calibration; no recommendation is “good” because it looks plausible.

## Capture strategy

The minimum, professional and supreme lanes are specified in the root report and canonical capture protocol. The critical product investment is not custom SLAM first; it is a capture companion that guarantees ordinary-file custody, calibration completeness, closed loops, coverage and before-leaving-site checks.

## Go/no-go gates

1. **Historical raw gate:** no claim beyond vendor-export mode without official RGB/LiDAR/calibration/rights.
2. **Pilot identity gate:** T-507 confirms only anchors 000/010/020/040 and excludes 049. This closes the bounded human decision, not full 0–48/release-node classification or externally authenticated identity; no full-pilot, metric, runtime or release authority exists until the remaining identity, rights, control and review evidence passes.
3. **Rights gate:** no Matterport/LCC/LCC2-derived training until contract-specific approval.
4. **Trainer gate:** T-514 completed only an authority-none local Config B contract preflight with decision `contract_valid_runtime_blocked`; it did not run or enable training. Actual splat training remains RunPod-only under D-016 and additionally requires pinned runtime/source closure, distorted-camera and depth-loss paths, bilateral/evaluation/resume/D-014 proof, passing stage-specific rights validation, independently clean activation, trusted `FoundryRightsApproval`, a subject-bound short-lived `FoundryExecutionConfirmation` and exact trusted compute approval.
5. **Metric gate:** no planning authority until control/alignment profile passes.
6. **Visual gate:** no “better” claim until blinded fixed-view and held-out metrics pass.
7. **Truth gate:** no generated region in captured/metric authority.
8. **Runtime gate:** no public promotion until source/runtime loss and device budgets pass.
9. **Provider gate:** no remote execution without an independently clean activation contract, validated provider plan, exact cost/cap/kill controls, current rights and identity authority, consumed confirmation, compute approval and parity/containment evidence.

## Strategic cross-pollination conclusions

- T-507 demonstrates reproducible same-lineage E57/COLMAP registration, so E57 is a strong candidate anchor for a bounded photo/video experiment; it does not establish independent control, a reviewed TransformArtifact, accuracy authority or runtime/public authority.
- LCC poses without frames can seed alignment/diagnosis but cannot train or validate a photo reconstruction by themselves.
- Supplemental owned DSLR images can create a residual/hero layer if camera registration and rights pass.
- Hero micro-splats are a high-value bounded experiment, not a substitute for the base venue frame.
- A LiDAR mesh can condition a cinematic model, but the model output remains non-metric.
- Semantic masks and geometry uncertainty can drive exact recapture.
- A single Foundry manifest can feed Spark, GLB, OpenUSD and future Khronos exporters because formats are adapters.
- Historical Matterport/Unreal artifacts may accelerate the pilot only after source identity/rights checks.
- Prospective open capture is cheaper and more dependable strategically than an unauthorized '.xbin' decoder.
