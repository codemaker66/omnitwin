# D-011 — Spatial confidence budget

Status: proposed
Date: 2026-04-25
Authors: Blake
Supersedes: none

## Context

Venviewer is a venue *planning* tool. Planners make load-bearing decisions on top of our renders: where the dance floor goes, whether the chandelier clears the truss, which doorway the band's gear fits through, how many guests fit at safe spacing. If the rendered geometry is wrong by 30 cm and the planner doesn't know, that error becomes their error. Per §687 of the deep research synthesis, formalizing what we know vs. what we've inferred, surfaced in the UI, is the highest-leverage architectural work available. Today nothing in the pipeline tells the user whether the visible geometry is survey-grade, layout-grade, or appearance-only.

## Decision

Every layer in the VSIR-0 graph (D-009) reports QA metrics:

- alignment RMS (mesh ↔ point cloud)
- ICP residuals (OBJ ↔ COLMAP)
- COLMAP reprojection error
- projection seam confidence (per panorama region)
- splat / base disagreement (residual delta)
- certification tier of the source capture (D-015)

These metrics are aggregated into a per-region Truth Heatmap rendered in the UI:

- **cyan** — ≤ 1 cm (survey-grade)
- **green** — 1–3 cm (ops-grade)
- **yellow** — 3–10 cm (layout-grade)
- **orange** — 10–30 cm (appearance-only with rough alignment)
- **red** — > 30 cm or splat-only with no LiDAR anchor (geometry is appearance-only)
- **greyscale** — photometric only (no measurable geometry)

Hard rule: the "Measure" tool refuses measurements in regions worse than `LAYOUT_GRADE_5CM`. No measurement leaves the system without its confidence tier tag attached.

## Consequences

Every operational handoff (BEO export, vendor pack, capacity certificates, fire-code documents) inherits the tier system — a measurement on a yellow-tier wall carries that uncertainty downstream. Planners stop treating the render as ground truth in regions that aren't. The pipeline gains a forcing function to keep alignment metrics current; if a region drifts to red, the UI shows it.

## Alternatives considered

- **Trust the render, no confidence layer** — rejected. Silent geometry errors become silent planner errors; load-bearing for legal/operational documents.
- **Single global confidence number per venue** — rejected. Hides regional variation that is exactly what planners need to see (the chandelier alcove may be cyan while the back-of-house corridor is orange).
- **Confidence in metadata only, not surfaced in UI** — rejected. The whole point is to make uncertainty visible to the planner at decision time.
