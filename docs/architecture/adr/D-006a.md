# D-006a — gsplat MCMC + bilateral grid with 3DGUT hedge

Status: proposed
Date: 2026-04-25
Authors: Blake
Supersedes: D-006

## Context

D-006 commits to gsplat MCMC + bilateral grid as the training stack. Deep research synthesis confirmed the choice for pinhole-rectified panorama captures but flagged that NVIDIA's 3DGUT (CVPR 2025 Oral, integrated in gsplat 1.5.2) supports distorted cameras (fisheye, rolling-shutter) natively in rasterization, which matters for incremental phone/360-camera capture of hard-to-reach venue regions. Local Windows training has also been deprecated in favor of RunPod A100 80GB due to environment fragility (gsplat 1.5.3 only on Python 3.10, NumPy 2.x patches, fused-ssim CUDA mismatch).

## Decision

Keep MCMC + bilateral grid for pinhole captures. Add 3DGUT as parallel training mode (`--with_ut --with_eval3d` in gsplat) for distorted-camera ingestion. RunPod is the canonical training environment; local training is deprecated.

## Consequences

Distorted-camera capture (Insta360, fisheye, phone) now has a viable training path. RunPod runbook (T-002) is dependency for any new training run. Existing Trades Hall splat baseline continues with MCMC + bilateral grid; no retroactive retraining.

## Alternatives considered

- **Keeping D-006 unchanged** — rejected. Locks training to pinhole; blocks 360-camera incremental capture.
- **Switching wholly to 3DGUT** — rejected. Loses MCMC quality on pinhole captures.
- **Switching trainer entirely** — rejected. Brush 0.3 was evaluated and rejected (see D-006 original); gsplat remains primary.
