IIHow we capture light

Two technologies, one room — captured the way you actually see it.

Most venue tools capture a venue once, with one technology, and accept its limits. We capture the Grand Hall twice — using two complementary methods, each of which excels where the other fails. Walls, floors and architecture are captured as precise geometry. Chandeliers, stained glass and polished surfaces are captured using a brand-new technique that records exactly how light moves and reflects, frame by frame. The two are layered together in real time. The result is a digital Grand Hall that behaves the way the real one does.

Built on3D Gaussian Splatting (a 2023 Stanford / INRIA breakthrough) + classical photogrammetric mesh.

IXThe luxury photorealism index

Off-the-shelf measures of quality don't measure what matters. So we built our own.

No existing measurement of digital twin quality captures what a venue planner actually cares about — does the chandelier look right, do the moldings read at full detail, does the polished floor reflect convincingly? So we built our own scoring framework, evaluated blind by independent reviewers, analysed with the statistical rigour of a clinical study. Trades Hall's Grand Hall will not be released for client use until it scores 4.6 or higher. The standard is set publicly, and held to.

Built onBlind evaluation methodology with Friedman test, region-restricted PSNR/SSIM/LPIPS metrics.
