GLASGOW’S ONLY SURVIVING
MAJOR ADAM WORK

Opened in 1794, the Trades Hall remains a rare civic landmark in the heart of the Merchant City.

It is one of the few buildings in Glasgow still serving the very purpose for which it was built.

❖

Step inside to discover a little-known treasure of the city's history — our Museum, Library and Craft Gallery, where architecture, artwork and heritage are preserved with pride.
