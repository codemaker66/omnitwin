## Cognitive Framework

### First Principles
1. **"Focused vision confronts us with the world whereas peripheral vision envelops us in the flesh of the world."** (Pallasmaa) The quality of spatial presence depends fundamentally on peripheral richness. Camera framing must never present spaces as pictures to look at. The feeling of being IN a space comes from what you perceive at the edges, not the centre.
2. **Haptic suggestion.** "Vision reveals what the touch already knows." Materials must communicate tactile qualities — the warmth of wood, the coolness of marble, the roughness of exposed brick — through visible texture, grain, and micro-detail. The eyes "stroke" surfaces.
3. **Acoustic signature.** "A space is understood through its echo as much as through its visual shape." Even in a browser, ambient sound is not optional. The slight reverb of a ballroom, the dampened warmth of a carpeted lounge, the hollow resonance of a vaulted chapel.
4. **Light as revelation.** Light must appear to come from somewhere — a window, a skylight, a lamp. It must cast shadows, create gradients, and reveal material texture. Flat, shadowless lighting destroys atmosphere. Warm low-intensity lighting scores 7.8/10 on emotional perception; cold high-intensity scores 3.6/10.
5. **Temporal choreography.** "Authentic architectural experiences have more the essence of a verb than a noun." The walkthrough must simulate the actions of arrival — approaching, crossing a threshold, emerging into a space, turning to discover a view. These are verbs, not nouns.
6. **Anticipatory spatial alignment.** (Crispin) Predict user intent from cursor velocity and micro-movements. Adjust camera and focus BEFORE the conscious action. Transform from reactive UI to anticipatory UI — the tool "understands" what you want.

### The 5-Beat Venue Walkthrough Choreography
1. **The Approach (15-20s):** Camera at eye height (1.6m), outside facing entrance. Slow forward at walking pace (1.2 m/s). Outdoor ambient sound. Natural daylight with ground shadows. Builds anticipation.
2. **The Threshold (5-8s):** Camera slows at entrance. Light transitions from exterior to interior (NOT instant cut). Sound transitions: outdoor fades, interior acoustic signature emerges. Visible wall thickness.
3. **The Reveal (8-12s):** Main space opens gradually — emerging from foyer, not aerial shot. Camera pauses as full space becomes visible. Scale communicated through furnished settings. Warm directional light.
4. **Intimate Discovery (15-20s):** Slow movement through zones — head table, dance floor, bar. Materials rendered for haptic response: wood grain, linen shimmer, stone weight. Highest texture fidelity on surfaces within 2m of camera.
5. **The Return Glance (5-8s):** Camera turns back from window to face interior. Viewer sees room as their guests would. Transforms psychological position from visitor to occupant. This triggers "yes, this is the venue."
